Strong and sturdy, with eight little pieces of annoyance. 2014年8月11日
评论者 Mr Brooks - 已在美国亚马逊上发表
已确认购买
Though I have no way to compare this to the previous model, I have nothing but love for this edition. It may be smaller and slightly out of proportion (the head seems too big for the body, and the scale is way off when compared to the minifigures), but it is incredibly well designed. Everything on this set is built to endure playful hands, with the exception of eight little things.

The disks on the joints fall off. Every time you move the legs, these eight little disks just pop off at random. I'm going to superglue mine on. It's honestly not that big of a problem, but I was going to make a stop motion video and that minor detail took away from the filming process. The disks don't affect the rigidity at all, but it's a little annoying.

Aside from that issue, everything else is superb. The legs are stiff and hold poses well. The spring loaded missiles fire about fifteen feet. Impressive really. The new minifigures are detailed, but I am not crazy about the snowtroopers' skirts. They are designed like the Jedi cloaks, but do not flow as well.

My favorite little detail is the hydraulic connection from the legs to the feet. And building the feet was fun. The entire set is a fun assembly, but the little details really make it worth while.

The entire assemble takes about four hours for an adult and most of the instructions are clear and easy to follow. The black pieces are now outlined in white, so no more struggling with the confusion of gray or black bricks. I ended up having about 25 spare pieces after the build, and nothing was missing.

All in all, I would not only recommend this set to others, but I am actually about to order a second set. It's seriously that fun. A must have for any Lego Star Wars fan. And at the new prices, it's a great deal compared to last year's lineup.

Towering figure makes a great decoration 2014年11月5日
评论者 padj650 - 已在美国亚马逊上发表
I picked this up at target along with the snowspeeder at a local Target on a whim a few weeks after buying & making the X-wing. The painfully big price tag aside (honestly, all Legos are going to cost more than what you want to pay these days), the set is pretty awesome when finished. I worked like a madman for about 2 hours to finish it (no stickers in this set), with 8 bags and 2 sets of instructions. Some parts like the legs are repeating/mirrored instructions so if you can figure out which those are, it'll save you some time by making the figure assembly line-style.

With the working trap door, opening cockpit and the opening torso(?) it makes for an awesome set that you can definitely recreate scenes from Episode V. The head/cockpit turns on a swivel but won't dangle loosely around, and spring loaded missiles fire surprisingly far (but not hard enough to injure someone by mistake). The legs are double jointed (one point where the leg joins the body, and one point at the "knee" of the leg) so you can pose it as if it's walking, and the snowspeeder's rope can wrap around two legs to bring it down. One thing you don't realize until you attach the legs onto the body is that the figure is really tall, given that most Star Wars figures are spaceships that are relatively short and long -- this one is tall and is great to look at. I don't imagine I'll be playing with these that much but it sure will make an awesome decoration with my X-wing and Snowspeeder.
